Tomato Soil Testing Guide

Four essential soil parameters for optimal tomato growth and yield

Soil pH Level

pH measures soil acidity or alkalinity, affecting nutrient availability for tomatoes.

Testing & Recommendations

  • Optimal Range: 6.0 - 6.8
  • Test with pH meter or test kit
  • If pH < 6.0: Add agricultural lime
  • If pH > 6.8: Add sulfur or peat moss

Nitrogen (N) Content

Essential for leaf and stem growth. Affects plant vigor and green color.

Testing & Recommendations

  • Optimal Range: 20-40 ppm
  • Test with soil test kit or lab
  • If low: Apply compost or blood meal
  • If high: Reduce nitrogen fertilizers

Phosphorus (P) Content

Crucial for root development, flowering, and fruit production in tomatoes.

Testing & Recommendations

  • Optimal Range: 30-50 ppm
  • Test with phosphorus test kit
  • If low: Add bone meal or rock phosphate
  • If high: Avoid phosphorus fertilizers

Potassium (K) Content

Important for fruit quality, disease resistance, and water regulation.

Testing & Recommendations

  • Optimal Range: 150-250 ppm
  • Test with potassium test kit
  • If low: Add wood ash or potash
  • If high: Reduce potassium fertilizers
