Micro Nutrients in Tomatoes

Essential trace elements for healthy tomato growth and high-quality fruit

Tomatoes require various micro nutrients in small quantities for optimal growth, disease resistance, and fruit quality. These essential elements play crucial roles in plant metabolism and development.

Zinc (Zn)

Essential for enzyme activation and protein synthesis. Zinc deficiency causes stunted growth and small leaves.

Application:

2-5 kg/acre as zinc sulfate. Apply during early growth stages.

Boron (B)

Important for cell wall formation and fruit development. Boron deficiency leads to fruit cracking and poor fruit set.

Application:

1-2 kg/acre as borax. Apply during flowering stage.

Iron (Fe)

Crucial for chlorophyll formation and energy transfer. Iron deficiency causes yellowing between leaf veins.

Application:

5-10 kg/acre as iron sulfate. Apply every 30 days.
